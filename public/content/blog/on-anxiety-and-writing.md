I had been using LLM a lot, and even come to study it before I understand it much well. Sometimes people say it's a fortune that they grasp writing and coding before LLM coming out; for me, I think I am also lucky being able to learn and decide how I wanted to use it, at least before it becoming a social convention. So I decided to write more myself in case I got my brain rotted.

It seems that I am trying to blame my not writing well and stucking in projects to LLM, which I know is an excuse. Much like people wishing to be more and more productive with the help of LLM, or under the "Fear of Missing Out" pressure, I've been consistently fear restless, multitasking, expecting to achieve more than peers.

The anxiety that finally pushed me here is hard to describe precisely. It is not the sharp kind that comes with a deadline. It is a sense that things are happening too fast, that I am moving through experiences without fully registering them, that I will look back and find gaps where there should be memory.

Writing, I think, is a way of critically examining what on earth kepting pushing me, even towards burnout. On the other hand, I still felt guilty for not writing my paper and assignment, and for keeping dragging task blocks in my calendar to the day after, but writing them down is at least a relief.

Ironically, this reflection looks better and more genenuine that the one I wrote for uni's intergrating course reflection (on LLM use), so I guess this (a bit of performantive) form of reflection can nudge me better on thinking and reflecting. I am trying not to plan it too carefully otherwise it may become another source of my anxiety. The point is to show up and write something, and see what accumulates. For example, I was building this personal website alongside my study and work (to put off my paper writing and revision), which looks not bad.
